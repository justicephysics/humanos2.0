## The Numbers That Cannot Be Ignored

By 2030, unless the architecture changes, AI data centres are projected to:

- **Consume 9.3 trillion litres of fresh water** — enough for the annual domestic needs of 1.3 billion people in Sub‑Saharan Africa.
- **Occupy 14,500 square kilometres of land** — a data‑centre footprint larger than many countries.
- **Demand 945 terawatt‑hours of electricity** — nearly triple the combined consumption of Pakistan, Bangladesh, and Nigeria, roughly 3 % of all global electricity.

And 80–90 % of that energy is spent on **inference** — the everyday use of AI models like ChatGPT, which alone processes 2.5 billion prompts per day and consumes around 383 GWh annually.

- **The Canal Dogma** keeps us believing that packing more into less space is the only path, even when the planet is screaming otherwise.
