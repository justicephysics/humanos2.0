## 2. Why Our Instruments See a Cube — The Internal Perspective

Every instrument we build is embedded within the Y‑plenum itself. We can only map the universe from the inside. And from the inside, the plenum appears **straight** in all directions.

This is because the Y‑plenum, in its minimum‑energy ground state at 2.7 K, forces every local connection between two nearby points to be a straight line. - **Three axes (X, Y, Z) are mutually perpendicular** — just like the edges of a cube.

This is the geometry we experience. This is why we call the universe “flat.” It is the natural, internal view of a plenum that is locally at peace. The cube is our local truth.

## 3. Why the True Shape Is a Sphere — The External Perspective (Impossible to See)

- **The total energy \(J\) is finite.** Therefore, the total volume \(A/V\) that contains it must also be finite. An infinite flat cube would require infinite energy, which contradicts the fundamental conservation we have established.
- **The plenum is closed.** It does not extend to infinity. Straight lines, followed far enough, eventually curve back to their starting point—not because the plenum is intrinsically bent at every location, but because the total volume is finite and self‑connected. This is the geometry of a sphere.
- **The sphere is the only shape where every point is equivalent.** There is no centre on the surface of a sphere; every point is equally the “centre.” In our 3‑dimensional case, every point in the cosmos is equally surrounded by the rest of the cosmos. The true “centre of mass” is not a point in the 3D space; it is the entire volume at the moment of the Big Bang—a phase when the whole arena was maximally compressed.

So the external shape is a sphere. But we can never photograph it, never map it, never step back to see it. The sphere is the global truth, hidden behind our local straightness.
