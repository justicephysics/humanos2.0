## 2. The Locked Puzzle Box — Why Hardware Alone Is Not Enough

An ASML lithography machine is not just a product. It is the culmination of a thirty‑year global supply chain, guarded by two impenetrable walls:

- **The Hardware Wall:** The mirrors, manufactured by Carl Zeiss in Germany, are the flattest surfaces ever created by humans. The light source fires molten tin droplets 50,000 times per second. The entire assembly requires thermal stabilization down to fractions of a degree. Even if a competitor perfectly duplicated every pipe, laser, and mirror, the machine would still be useless.
- **The Software Wall:** Every physical component is calibrated against the others by proprietary ASML algorithms. Without the calibration code, the machine cannot print a single chip. The hardware is the lock; the software is the key. And the key is owned by ASML alone.

This is the Canal Dogma in its purest physical form: **the narrow gate is not just economic or educational; it is embedded in the very machines that produce computation.**
