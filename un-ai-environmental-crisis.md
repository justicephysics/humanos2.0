## The UN's Blunt Warning

The United Nations University has released a stark report on the environmental footprint of artificial intelligence. The numbers are no longer predictions; they are unfolding realities:

- **Electricity:** AI-related workloads accounted for 20% of data center electricity in 2025, projected to double to **40% by 2030**. AI's global electricity consumption could power all of sub‑Saharan Africa for over two years, generating a carbon footprint of **400 million tons of CO₂**.
- **Water:** Data centers are projected to consume a colossal **9.3 trillion liters of water annually** for cooling by 2030 — enough to satisfy the drinking water needs of the entire global population for more than a year and a half.
- **Land and Waste:** The rapid expansion is creating a massive pileup of up to **2.5 million tons of annual electronic waste** by 2030. Over 150 nations have no sovereign AI capacity while they bear the brunt of mineral extraction and waste.
- **Digital Divide:** Currently, 90% of specialized AI cloud infrastructure is concentrated in just the United States and China, leaving the rest of the world dependent and exposed.

UN experts are urging governments, developers, and everyday users to embed environmental impacts into all AI decisions, emphasizing that even 'renewable‑powered' data centers carry heavy environmental footprints that cannot be ignored.
