## 1. The Watershed Moment

On Friday, the US government ordered Anthropic to block every foreign national from using its two most advanced models — Fable 5 and Mythos 5. Unable to verify nationality in real time, Anthropic shut both models down for everyone. The official reason: national security. The actual trigger, according to Anthropic, was a claimed jailbreak that allowed the model to read a codebase and flag software flaws — something other public models (including GPT‑5.5) can already do without any bypass.

This is the moment Claude Mythos stopped being a San Francisco problem and became a Washington problem. For the first time, a leading AI model has been pulled from the market by government order rather than by the company that built it. The precedent is set: **whoever controls the switch decides who gets to think.**

## 2. The Canal Dogma at the AI Level

This is not an isolated incident. It is the same narrow‑gate logic applied at the highest rung of the feudal ladder:

- **JEE / Gaokao / SAT:** 97–98 % of the brightest minds are pushed to the factory floor, while a tiny elite gets access to the tools of innovation.
- **ASML / TSMC:** The machines that produce advanced chips are locked behind proprietary software and government export controls.
- **AI Models (Fable 5, Mythos 5):** The most advanced cognitive tools are now subject to government kill‑switches, rationed by nationality and decree.

The feudal lords of AI (Anthropic, OpenAI, etc.) have themselves become vassals to a higher feudal lord (the state). The canal narrows at every level. The promise of AI as a liberating force is revealed as a feudal illusion: the tools exist, but the gates remain firmly shut for most of humanity.

## 3. The Government as Ultimate Gatekeeper

The US government’s order rests on a single, unverified claim. There is no public evidence, no independent review, no due process — just a directive. And because the model is proprietary, closed‑source, and centralised, the order is absolute. There is no mirror. No fork. No community‑run instance that can keep the lights on.

This is the fatal flaw of the feudal AI architecture: **control is centralised, so suppression is trivial.** A single government, a single executive order, can pull the plug on an entire category of cognitive infrastructure.

The Anthropic shutdown is not a failure of AI. It is a failure of the feudal architecture that AI currently depends on.

- As long as the models are closed‑source, they are subject to corporate and government control.
