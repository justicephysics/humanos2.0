## 1. The Two Zero‑Sum Games

A zero‑sum game is simple: the total is fixed. What one gains, another loses. The sum is zero.

The universe operates on this principle absolutely. Energy is conserved. $$E = MC^2$$ tells us mass can convert to energy, but the total remains constant. Nothing is created. Nothing is destroyed.

But there are two fundamentally different ways to play a zero‑sum game:

| Method | Mechanism | Outcome |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Distribution** | Energy is spread across the entire system. Every node receives what it needs. The total is conserved. | Stability. Circulation. Thriving. The cosmos has run for 13.8 billion years. |
| **Extraction** | Energy is concentrated into a few nodes. The majority are drained. The total is conserved. | Pressure. Heat. Collapse. Every empire has fallen. The Fortune 500 is next. |

The arithmetic is identical. The architecture is opposite.

**The cosmic zero‑sum game is a circulation engine.** Energy moves. It is subtracted from one region and added to another. The total never changes. The system never overheats. The cosmos has never collapsed.
