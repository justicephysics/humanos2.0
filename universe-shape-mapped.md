## 4. The Cosmic Geometry — A Ball of Straight Lines

Imagine the centre of mass of the universe—a vast, dense core of all matter. From this centre, the Y‑plenum radiates outward in every direction. At every point, the plenum is straight, because straightness is its minimum‑energy state. A line drawn at 90 degrees to the plenum’s tension follows the direction of maximum relaxation. It never bends back on itself locally.

But because the total volume is finite, these straight lines do not extend to infinity. They eventually reach the limit of the plenum—not a wall, but a closure, where the geometry curves back on itself, completing the sphere. From within, we see only the straightness—the cube. From without, we would see the sphere—the perfect, finite, boundaryless whole.

This is why General Relativity uses curvature to describe gravity near matter. The plenum itself is straight. But the pull of matter changes the positions of matter within the plenum. When we map those changing positions with our mathematical tools, the paths appear curved. The map is curved; the territory is straight. Both are true. Both are necessary.
