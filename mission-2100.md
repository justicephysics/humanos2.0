## The Mission

**Mission 2100** is not a corporate roadmap. It is a civilizational campaign — a deliberate, physics‑grounded, multi‑generational engineering project to move humanity from the old extractive canals into the open, cold, light‑connected cosmos.

We have three primary targets.

### Target 1 — Reach Beyond 5 % of the Ocean

Today, less than 5 % of the ocean has been explored or utilized in a clean, sustainable way. The oceans are a vast, cold, resource‑rich reservoir — the Earth's own 2.7 K lattice analogue.

- With Light@0Kelvin computation and free energy from fusion or orbital solar, we can decode and use the oceans without extraction.
- Desalination, mineral harvesting, thermal energy conversion, carbon capture — all become solvable engineering problems, not political debates.
- The ocean becomes a partner, not a dumping ground.

### Target 2 — Reach Beyond in Space, Where We Are Now

The current space presence — ISS, Chinese space station, satellites — is a foothold. Mission 2100 transforms it into a permanent, self‑sustaining, productive infrastructure.

- **Orbital compute fabrics** — Unit 1 (sun‑shield + power) and Unit 2 (thin‑wide, photonic, cryogenic modules) — provide unlimited, zero‑operating‑cost computation.
- **Orbital energy farms** — solar panels in permanent sunlight, beaming power to Earth or to other spacecraft.
- **Space‑based manufacturing** — microgravity enables perfect crystals, alloys, and pharmaceuticals.
- The cosmic playground becomes the engine of a new economy.

### Target 3 — M2M²: March to Moon and Mars, and Beyond

M2M² = March to Moon and Mars, and create a permanent base to move beyond.

- **Moon** — a permanent, self‑sustaining base. Water from the poles, regolith for construction, low gravity for launch to deeper space.
- **Mars** — a second planetary home, terraformed or habitat‑based, serving as a testbed for closed‑loop ecosystems.
- **Beyond** — asteroid mining, outer‑planet moons, and eventually interstellar probes powered by Light@0Kelvin photonic propulsion.

This is not a flag‑planting exercise. It is the expansion of the human canal into the cosmos — wide, distributed, and cold.

None of this will be built by the old feudal system. ASML, TSMC, NVIDIA, Samsung — these are not our enemies; they are **stations to pass through**, not our mission.

### Who Are the Warrior‑Engineers?

They are the children of our common people — not the elite, not the 0.3 %, not the graduates of narrow gates.

- They are the millions who would have been crushed by the JEE, Gaokao, SAT — if the canal remained narrow.
- They are the students who failed the exam but never lost their curiosity.
- They are the factory workers, farmers, and dreamers who understand the physical world better than any textbook.
- They are the ones who need only a **survival kit** — basic livelihood, a thin client, and an open‑source education — to become a force of nature.

### How We Transform Them

- **From survival mode to warrior mode.** Today's common people fight to survive. Tomorrow's Warrior‑Engineers fight to build.
- **Open‑source knowledge.** The Virtual Nalanda provides the complete blueprint — free, global, no entrance exam.
- **Real‑world projects.** Thin‑wide chip prototypes, village transformations, CubeSat deployments — learning by building.
- **A shared mission.** They are not working for a stock option; they are liberating their countries and their planet from technological feudalism.

### The Multiplier Effect

Less chicken, less egg — that's the feudal model. ASML restricts the tools; TSMC restricts the fabs; the narrow gate restricts the talent. The result: a slow, hot, expensive trickle of innovation.

Millions of Warrior‑Engineers flip the equation:

- **More engineers → more ideas → more prototypes → more breakthroughs → more engineers.**
- A multiplier effect at mind‑boggling speed.
- The feudal lords cannot compete with an open‑source hurricane.
