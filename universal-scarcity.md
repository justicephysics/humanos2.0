## 1. The Scarcity Lie

Every policy that narrows the canal—every exam gate, every interest rate hike, every austerity measure—is justified by a single claim: **"Resources are scarce. We must ration them."**

But if resources are truly scarce, the scarcity must be **universal**. It must apply to the top 10% exactly as it applies to the bottom 50%. The billionaire must feel the same pinch as the farmer. The central banker must live within the same constraints as the student debtor.

**This is not what we observe.**

| **Bottom 50%** | 2% of wealth. | Struggle for food, shelter, education, medicine. Told to "live within their means." |

The scarcity is not universal. It is **manufactured**. It is a policy choice designed to concentrate resources at the top and impose austerity on the rest. The claim of "scarce resources" is the justification for the narrow canal—and it is a lie.

## 2. The Logic — If Scarcity Were Real

Let us apply a simple test: **the universality principle.**

- If food is scarce, then no one should have the excess to be obese while others starve.
- If water is scarce, then no one should have private swimming pools while villages go dry.
- If energy is scarce, then no one should burn fuel for joyrides while hospitals lack power.
- If money is scarce, then no one should have a trillion‑dollar net worth while students drown in debt.

The mere existence of extreme luxury alongside extreme deprivation is the **proof that scarcity is artificial**. The feudal lords do not live within the constraints they impose on others. The canal is narrow only at the bottom; at the top, it is a floodplain of excess.
