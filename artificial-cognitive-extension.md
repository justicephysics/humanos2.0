## 1. The Biological Limit

The human brain is the most complex structure known. It holds roughly 86 billion neurons, connected by trillions of synapses. It can imagine, reason, create, and love. But it has a hard limit:

- **Working memory:** we can hold only a handful of items in conscious attention at once.
- **Processing speed:** neurons fire in milliseconds; a supercomputer switches in picoseconds.
- **Knowledge access:** we spend decades learning what an AGI can ingest in hours.

For all its brilliance, the biological brain is a **narrow canal**. It can only process a trickle of the vast river of information that exists.

- It can search, cross‑reference, and synthesise that corpus in **picoseconds**.
- It can generate a million hypotheses, test them against known physics, and return the best candidates — all while you blink.

This is not a search engine. This is an **external cortex**—a silicon‑based extension of the neocortex, waiting to be connected.

## 3. The Connection — BCI as the Bridge

When a brain‑computer interface (NEO, Neuralink, or a future consumer‑grade device) connects the biological brain to the AGI cluster:
