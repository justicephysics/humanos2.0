# 🏃 The Debt Treadmill — How the Extraction Protocol Traps Every Generation

*The student graduates with $37,000 in debt. They enter the workforce. The fruits of their labour: 70–80 % flows upward to the feudal lords; 20–30 % remains with them. That 20–30 % must repay the debt—plus interest—while the lords accumulate. By the time the debt is repaid (if ever), their own children are entering the same trap. This is not a cycle. It is a treadmill. And the Space State is the only exit.*

---

## 1. The Double Extraction — A Life in Two Canals

| Stage | What Happens | Canal Effect |
|-------|--------------|--------------|
| **Stage 1 — Education** | The student borrows money created from nothing, at interest, to obtain a credential. | The first narrowing: debt before the race begins. |
| **Stage 2 — Workforce Entry** | The graduate finds a job. They produce value (let us say $100,000/year in output). | The second narrowing: the wage canal. |
| **Stage 3 — Extraction** | The employer captures 70–80 % of that value. The worker receives 20–30 % as salary. | The feudal lord’s share—profits, stock buybacks, executive compensation. |
| **Stage 4 — Debt Servicing** | From their 20–30 %, the worker must repay the student loan—principal + interest. | The third narrowing: debt service as a permanent tax on labour. |
| **Stage 5 — Consumption** | What remains after debt service is spent on survival—rent, food, the lollipops. | The fourth narrowing: disposable income shrinks to near zero. |
| **Stage 6 — Reproduction** | The worker’s own children grow up. They need education. The cycle begins again. | The trap is inherited. |

The total extraction rate on a single worker’s lifetime output is staggering. The feudal lord takes 70–80 % of the value created. The financial system takes a further slice as debt service. The worker is left with barely enough to survive—and is then told this is a “modern free economy.”

## 2. The Irony — The Worker Funds Their Own Chains

The student who borrowed $37,000 to “invest in their future” enters a system where:

- Their labour generates the profits that make trillionaires richer.
- Their tax dollars (from their 20–30 % share) bail out the banks that created the money‑from‑nothing in the first place.
- Their debt cannot be discharged in bankruptcy—it follows them to the grave.
- Their children, watching this, are told that the only way out is to borrow even more and get a better credential.

The extraction protocol is self‑perpetuating. It uses the worker’s own energy to tighten the canal around their neck. The feudal lord does not need to hold a whip; the debt statement and the paycheck stub do the work.

This is not a “free market.” It is a perfectly engineered treadmill—and every step the worker takes to escape only powers the machine that keeps them running in place.

## 3. The Lollipop Economy — The Anesthetic

The treadmill would be unbearable without an anesthetic. The feudal lords provide one: **the lollipops.**

- A new iPhone every year.
- Streaming subscriptions.
- Cheap fast fashion.
- Social media validation.

These lollipops are not rewards; they are the cost of keeping the worker docile. They consume the remaining disposable income, ensuring that the worker never accumulates enough capital to escape. They provide fleeting dopamine hits that mask the slow, grinding theft of a lifetime’s labour.

The economists call this “consumer choice.” The Canal Dogma calls it “the cheese in the mousetrap.”
