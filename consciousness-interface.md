## 1. The State of Brain‑Computer Interfaces

The race to commercialise brain‑computer interfaces (BCIs) is accelerating:

- **China’s NEO chip** (Tsinghua University / Neuracle Technology) has been approved for clinical use — the world’s first commercially available brain implant. It sits between the skull and the brain, is less invasive than penetrating electrodes, and has already been tested in 36 patients.
- **Elon Musk’s Neuralink** is in human trials in the United States, aiming to restore movement and sight. Musk calls it “Jesus‑level technology.”

Both devices aim to help patients with paralysis, spinal cord injuries, and neurological disorders. The long‑term vision, however, is far more ambitious: direct communication between the human brain and external machines. Thought‑driven typing. Cursor control without hands. Eventually, digital telepathy.

But this focus misses something far more urgent and universal: **interfacing not with a computer, but with your own consciousness.**

By the time the harmful action occurs (the angry outburst, the corrupt deal, the invasion of Poland), C is already too low for the actor to perceive the harm they are about to cause.

**What if a brain‑computer interface could detect the rise of D — and alert the user before the command centre became isolated?**
