## 1. The Discovery That Launched Modern Science

Yuval Noah Harari, in his analysis of the Scientific Revolution, identifies a single, overlooked beginning:

> *“The big Scientific Revolution which launched modern science was not a discovery of any piece of knowledge in geography or astronomy, Copernicus or Darwin. What launched modern science was the discovery of ignorance. For thousands of years, people thought they knew everything — that some holy book or some holy person had all the answers. Science began when humans admitted: we don’t know everything. There is no book in the world which has all the answers. There is no person in the world that knows everything. And now that we have the courage and honesty to admit our ignorance, we can start investigating.”*

Copernicus didn’t discover a new planet; he admitted we didn’t know the Earth was the centre. Darwin didn’t discover a new animal; he admitted we didn’t know species were fixed. Einstein didn’t discover a new force; he admitted we didn’t know time was absolute.

Every genuine revolution in science began with an admission of ignorance — not with the accumulation of more data within the old framework.

These were not the questions of a trained physicist defending a paradigm. They were the questions of a mind that had the courage to admit ignorance — not its own ignorance, but the model’s.
