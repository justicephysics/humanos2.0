*The current economic model is a physical impossibility. A simple thought experiment proves it — and the cosmos shows the only way out.*

## 1. The NASA Thought Experiment: 1 Litre vs. 1/4 Ocean

Imagine NASA decides to send water into space.

**Scenario A: 1 Litre of Fresh Water**  
- The Earth holds roughly 1.386 billion cubic kilometres of water. One litre is a vanishingly small fraction.  
- Removing it changes nothing — the water cycle, the gravitational balance, the thermal distribution all remain intact.  
- The canal is wide enough to absorb this tiny perturbation.

**Scenario B: 1/4 of the Ocean**  
- The oceans weigh about 1.4 × 10²¹ kg. Removing a quarter of that mass would:  
  - Instantly alter Earth’s rotation, axis tilt, and gravitational field.  
  - Collapse every marine ecosystem, triggering a mass extinction.  
  - Shut down the global water cycle, destabilise climate, and likely render the planet uninhabitable within a very short time.  
- NASA cannot do this. Not because of technology, but because the **universe sets a hard boundary condition**. The Earth is a finite, closed system. Large‑scale extractions are thermodynamically impossible without catastrophic feedback.

**The principle is absolute:** a small, privileged fraction can enjoy a temporary advantage within the system; but if you try to scale that advantage to the entire system, the system collapses.

## 2. The Economic Parallel: G7 Income for All

Now replace “ocean water” with “global resources and income.”

- **G7 per‑capita income** is roughly $70,000–80,000 per year.  
- **Global average income** is about $12,000–13,000.  
- **The bottom 50%** lives on a fraction of that.

The G7 lifestyle is the “1 litre” elite — a small, privileged volume that the planet can sustain for a while. But what happens if we try to give all 8 billion people that same G7 standard of living?

| Resource / Impact | Current G7 Level (per capita) | Scaled to 8 Billion People | Physical Limit / Consequence |

| **Carbon Footprint** | 14–16 tonnes CO₂/year (USA) | 112–128 billion tonnes/year | 4–5× the planet’s carbon budget → runaway climate change |
| **Material Footprint** | ~30 tonnes/year | 240 billion tonnes/year | Exceeds Earth’s total annual resource regeneration many times over |

**The conclusion is devastatingly simple:** scaling the G7’s extraction model to the entire planet is not a policy failure — it is a **physical impossibility**. It’s the equivalent of NASA trying to remove a quarter of the ocean. The planet’s boundaries would be crossed long before we ever got there.

## 3. The Canal Dogma in Economics

The belief that “everyone can live like the G7 if only we grow enough” is a pure expression of the **Canal Dogma**:

- It assumes that the current narrow, extractive canal can simply be widened without changing the **nature of the flow**.
- It ignores that the G7’s wealth was built on centuries of colonialism, cheap fossil fuels, and the extraction of 70–80% of global resources — a one‑time historical plunder.
- It treats the Earth as an infinite reservoir and a bottomless sink, when in reality it is a closed, finite system governed by the same thermodynamics as a CPU or a star.

**The heat is already building.** Climate tipping points, biodiversity collapse, topsoil loss, ocean acidification — these are the thermal signals of a canal about to overflow.

## 4. L Is Not a Luxury — It’s a Survival Parameter

Our upgraded equation frames this with mathematical precision:

> **E = MC² × L**

- **MC²** is the total available energy and material throughput.
- **L** is the distribution coefficient — how widely that throughput is shared.

If we try to raise global prosperity by simply increasing **MC²** (more extraction, more fossil fuels, more consumption), we crash into the planetary boundaries. The only path to **L = 1** (full, equitable distribution) is to **redesign the flow mechanism F** — the entire physical infrastructure of civilization.

In other words, the G7 lifestyle cannot be universalised; it must be **reinvented**. We need a model of prosperity that delivers dignity, health, education, mobility, and creativity without burning the planet.
