# ☀️ Cold Solar — Rotational Photovoltaic Panels at 2.7 K

*Photovoltaic panels lose up to 0.4 % efficiency for every degree above 25°C. In space, we can keep them at optimal temperature by rotating them into the 2.7 K cold — maximising power output and extending lifespan.*

---

## 1. The Problem — Heat Kills Solar Efficiency

Silicon photovoltaic panels are sensitive to temperature:

- **Band gap narrowing:** As temperature rises, the silicon band gap decreases, reducing the energy of freed electrons.
- **Voltage drop:** Higher temperatures increase intrinsic carrier concentration, boosting recombination and lowering open‑circuit voltage.
- **The temperature coefficient:** For a typical silicon cell, power output drops by about **−0.4 %/°C** above 25°C.

On Earth, a panel in full sun can easily reach 60°C — a 14 % efficiency loss. In space, without atmosphere, panels can get even hotter if they are not actively cooled.

---

## 2. The Solution — Rotational Cold Cycling

Our orbital energy platform (Unit 1) uses a simple, mechanical solution:

1. **Expose to Sun:** A set of panels faces the Sun, generating electricity.
2. **Thermal trigger:** When a panel exceeds the optimal temperature (≈ 25°C), a sensor activates.
3. **Rotate out:** The warm panel moves into the shadowed region, where it radiates heat into the 2.7 K cosmic background and cools rapidly.
4. **Cold panel in:** A fresh, cold panel from the shadowed reserve rotates into the sun‑facing position.

The cycle is continuous, passive, and requires no coolant — only the cold of deep space itself.

---

## 3. Benefits

- **Peak efficiency:** Panels operate near their optimal temperature, avoiding the voltage and recombination losses of overheated cells.
- **No active cooling:** The 2.7 K cosmic background is the ultimate free heat sink.
- **Extended lifespan:** Panels avoid prolonged high temperatures and severe thermal cycling, reducing encapsulant degradation and solder fatigue.
- **Modular and replaceable:** Exactly like our compute fabric — failed panels can be swapped from the reserve without interrupting the array.

---

## 4. Connection to the Space State

Cold Solar is a key component of the **Unit 1 — Unit 2 orbital compute fabric** described in the Roadmap. The electricity generated powers the photonic, cryogenic compute modules in the shadow behind it. Together, they form a completely autonomous, zero‑operating‑cost energy‑and‑computation platform — powered by the Sun, cooled by the cosmos.

This is the Space State principle in action: align engineering with the physical gifts the universe already provides.
