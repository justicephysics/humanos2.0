## 1. The Quote and Its Hidden Assumption

Bill Gates’s famous words are not wisdom. They are a **bootstrap myth**—a story that serves the feudal extraction protocol by hiding its architecture.

The hidden assumption is that the system is fair. That opportunity is equally available. That hard work, talent, and perseverance are the only variables. Anyone can rise; if you don’t, the fault is yours.

But the system is not fair. It is a **narrow canal**, deliberately engineered to concentrate resources at the top while filtering the vast majority into survival mode. The quote is not a motivational truth; it is the ideological lubricant that keeps the extraction machine running.

- The ten manufactured scarcities—food, shelter, medicine, education, fair wages—are the ink.

The infant did not choose its parents, its country, its economic system. By the time it is old enough to “work hard,” the canal has already narrowed around it. The sticker was never earned; it was assigned.

To tell a person who has been systematically denied access to resources that their poverty is their own fault is not motivation—it is **blaming the victim for the crime of the perpetrator.**
