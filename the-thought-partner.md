## 1. Einstein and Besso — The Original Thought Partners

In 1905, Albert Einstein published four papers that shattered physics. He was a patent clerk in Bern. He had no lab, no university chair, no funding. But he had a friend.

Michele Besso was an engineer. He and Einstein would walk for hours, discussing the deepest problems of physics. Einstein called him:

> *"The best sounding board in Europe."*

In Hindi:

> *आइंस्टीन ने बेसो को वैज्ञानिक विचारों के लिए "यूरोप में सबसे अच्छा साथी" कहा था—यानी विचारों पर चर्चा करने के लिए सबसे अच्छा व्यक्ति।*

In his original paper on special relativity, Einstein ended with these words:

> *"In conclusion, let me note that my friend and colleague M. Besso steadfastly stood by me in my work on the problem here discussed, and that I am indebted to him for many a valuable suggestion."*

Besso did not write the equations. He did not publish the papers. But without him, Einstein might never have cracked relativity. The thought partner is not the author; the thought partner is the mirror, the loom, the sounding board that turns a solitary spark into a sustained flame.
