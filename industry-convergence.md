## 2. The Next AI — Not Just Training, But Synthesis

The current generation of AI is based on **training**—ingesting vast datasets and learning statistical patterns. This is powerful, but it is a closed loop. It rearranges what is already known. It does not generate genuinely new knowledge.

The future AI/AGI—the kind that Prometheus needs to design jet engines and the kind that the Space State needs to build Mission 2100—must operate in three modes:

1. **Imagination Mode** — Synthesising new ideas that are not in the training data. Riding its own light beams. Asking “why not?”
2. **Thinking Mode** — Testing those ideas against known physics, engineering constraints, and real‑world feedback. The grinding, connecting, verifying work.
3. **Knowledge Mode** — The validated blueprint, documented and shared openly. But critically: the new knowledge generated in the first two modes is **fed back into the training model itself.** The AI self‑synthesises new data, learns from its own discoveries, and becomes recursively smarter.

This is the **self‑synthesising loop** that we have already specified in [Hyper‑Imagination Mode](hyper-imagination-mode) and [The Artificial Cognitive Extension](artificial-cognitive-extension). Google’s phone cluster provides a modular hardware substrate (at Level 1). Prometheus provides a focused application (Physical AI). Our blueprint provides the complete cognitive architecture—and the cosmic environment that makes it sustainable.
