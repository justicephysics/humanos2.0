## The Invisible Throne

The Fortune 500 seat is not earned by merit alone. It is secured by a system that:

- **Creates artificial scarcity** — narrow exam gates, wage suppression, resource monopolies.
- **Concentrates the gains** — 70–80 % of the value created by workers, students, and farmers flows upward to the feudal lords.
- **Perpetuates the debt cycle** — students borrow to learn, workers borrow to survive, nations borrow to develop, and the lords collect the interest.

The student in debt, the family on rations, the factory worker in the smog — they are not separate tragedies. They are the **foundation** on which the Fortune 500 throne is built. The Canal Dogma ensures that the canal remains narrow enough to keep the serfs desperate, and wide enough at the top to let the lords swim in excess.

## The Fortune 500 Is a Mousetrap at Civilizational Scale

As we documented in [the-mousetrap](the-mousetrap.md) the trap works by attraction, not force. The Fortune 500 offers the cheese: a stable paycheck, a prestigious title, a comfortable retirement. But the cheese is paid for by the serfs' debt, hunger, and sweat.

The feudal lord does not need to hold a whip. The system holds it for him — the exam, the loan, the ration card, the factory gate. The serf polices themselves, believing that if they just work a little harder, they too might one day get a seat at the table.

**But the table is full.** The Fortune 500 is not expanding its seats for the serfs. It is reserving them for the lords.
