## 3. The Computational Canal — The CPU/GPU Traffic Jam

The same physics governs our processors.

- **Narrow channel:** Billions of transistors crammed into a tiny, dense silicon die. Copper wires form resistive, narrow data paths.
- **Resistance and heat:** More data pushed through → electrons collide with atoms → resistance → heat. As transistors shrink, leakage current increases, and the heat density worsens.
- **Canal overflow:** Thermal throttling. Skyrocketing cooling costs. The only way to maintain speed is to add more GPUs, which generate more heat, requiring more cooling — a snake‑and‑ladder game.

The current architecture is a canal‑overflow waiting to happen, and it already limits every supercomputer and data center on Earth.

**Our engineering cure (the 0 K Photonic Compute Fabric):**

No more traffic jam. No more canal overflow.
