## 1. The Research Breakthrough

A team of scientists has published a demonstration of a non‑volatile switching element that changes state in **~40 picoseconds** — roughly 40 trillionths of a second. Conventional semiconductor logic operates in the sub‑nanosecond range; high‑end CPU clock cycles are orders of magnitude slower once pipeline and memory effects are accounted for.

**How it works:**
- An ultrafast optical pulse is routed through a photodetector.
- The pulse triggers a change in electron spin states within a magnetic material stack (Mn₃Sn / tantalum on silica).
- The switching event encodes information.
- Once the state flips, it remains stable without continuous power — it is **non‑volatile**.

**The key insight from the researchers:** *“If switching can occur without sustained current, the theoretical benefit is not just speed but reduced energy per operation. That is the metric that actually matters at scale.”*
